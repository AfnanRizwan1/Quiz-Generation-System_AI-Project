## 1. Albert Einstein

```
Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany. He showed an early interest in mathematics and physics from a very young age. His teachers often described him as a slow learner, but he was simply bored by conventional teaching methods.

Einstein developed the theory of relativity, which completely changed the way scientists understood space, time, and gravity. His famous equation E=mc² showed that energy and mass are equivalent and interchangeable. He published his special theory of relativity in 1905, a year he called his miracle year because he produced four groundbreaking scientific papers.

In 1921, Einstein won the Nobel Prize in Physics for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect. He moved to the United States in 1933 when the Nazi party rose to power in Germany. Einstein worked at Princeton University until his death on April 18, 1955.
```

## 2. Marie Curie

```
Marie Curie was born on November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland. As a young girl, she was not allowed to attend university in Poland because women were banned from higher education at the time. She moved to Paris, France, where she studied physics and mathematics at the Sorbonne University.

Marie Curie discovered two new chemical elements: polonium, which she named after her home country, and radium. She conducted her research in a small, poorly equipped laboratory with very limited resources. Her work was extremely dangerous because she did not know that radioactive materials could harm the human body.

Curie won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903, sharing it with her husband Pierre Curie. In 1911, she won a second Nobel Prize in Chemistry, becoming the first person ever to win two Nobel Prizes. Marie Curie died on July 4, 1934, from aplastic anaemia caused by her long exposure to radiation.
```

## 3. Nelson Mandela

```

Mandela became the leader of the African National Congress Youth League and fought against the apartheid system in South Africa. He was arrested in 1962 and was sentenced to life imprisonment in 1964. He spent 27 years in prison on Robben Island before being released in 1990.

Mandela was elected as the first black President of South Africa in 1994. He won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993, sharing it with F.W. de Klerk. Mandela served as President until 1999 and died on December 5, 2013, at the age of 95.
```

## 4. The Eiffel Tower

```
The Eiffel Tower is located in Paris, France, on the Champ de Mars near the Seine River. It was built by Gustave Eiffel and his engineering company between 1887 and 1889. The tower was constructed as the entrance arch for the 1889 World's Fair, which celebrated the centennial of the French Revolution.

The Eiffel Tower stands 330 metres tall and was the tallest man-made structure in the world for 41 years. It consists of approximately 18,000 iron parts held together by 2.5 million rivets. The tower was originally painted red-brown but has been repainted in various shades over the years.

The Eiffel Tower was almost demolished in 1909 when its original permit expired. It was saved because it was useful as a radio transmission tower. Today, the Eiffel Tower is the most visited paid monument in the world, attracting nearly 7 million visitors every year.
```
